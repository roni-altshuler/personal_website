## Problem

Cell segmentation on whole-embryo zebrafish MERFISH images is the gating step for every downstream spatial-transcriptomics analysis. The out-of-the-box Cellpose model performed at an **F1 of ~53%** on our images — densely packed cells, thin membranes, heterogeneous tissue contexts, and channel noise all hurt boundary recall. Anything we built on top of that segmentation inherited its errors.

The team needed a segmentation pipeline that was accurate enough to trust for transcript assignment, and a training-data strategy that scaled — manually annotating every cell in a multi-gigabyte embryo image was not viable.

## Approach

<TwoColumn>
  <div>
    **Model.** I trained a custom Cellpose model on zebrafish-specific training data, tuning the diameter, flow threshold, and cellprob parameters against held-out crops.
  </div>
  <div>
    **Training data selection.** I built a sampling strategy based on **Shannon entropy** across image patches — high-entropy regions tend to contain rare cell types, dense boundaries, and edge cases that disproportionately improve generalization.
  </div>
</TwoColumn>

The entropy-driven sampling meant we got more model improvement per labeled patch, which is what made the project tractable at MERFISH scale.

## Results

<MetricCallout value="85%" label="F1 score" from="53%" />

The custom model hit an **F1 of 85%**, up from the 53% baseline — a 32-point absolute lift. Precision and recall both improved without sacrificing the other; boundary localization on thin membranes was the largest win.

Downstream impact: with reliable segmentation, transcript-to-cell assignment became trustworthy enough for spatial differential expression and cell-type clustering across the embryo.

## Tabula Sapiens Rosetta Donor

In parallel, I contributed to the **Tabula Sapiens Rosetta Donor** project, integrating isoform-level information with single-cell gene expression analyses. Isoforms are typically lost in standard single-cell workflows; bringing them back enables more precise cell-type and cell-state distinctions for the atlas.

## Downstream: cell types across the whole embryo

With segmentation trustworthy, transcript-to-cell assignment fed straight into clustering and cell-type annotation across the entire embryo.

## What I'd do next

- Push the model further with active-learning rounds where it flags its own low-confidence regions.
- Compare against transformer-based segmentation backbones (e.g. SAM-derived) on the same MERFISH dataset.
- Publish the entropy-sampling strategy — it's underused outside imaging and could generalize.
