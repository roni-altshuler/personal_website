## Overview

Two consecutive summers at CRISPR Therapeutics — first on the Autoimmune team (2022), then on the CRISPR-X team (2023). Both projects were wet-lab heavy with rigorous flow cytometry and NGS readouts. The two summers together gave me end-to-end exposure to therapeutic gene editing: target validation, payload design, edit verification, and functional cell-based assays.

## 2023 — CRISPR-X: improving knock-in efficiency

The hardest part of CRISPR/Cas9 for therapeutics is often not the cut — it's getting the desired sequence to integrate at the cut site. My project investigated **3'-overhang dsDNA donors** as a way to push HDR knock-in efficiency higher.

<TwoColumn>
  <div>
    **Design.** Constructed dsDNA donor templates with overhang structures targeting specific genomic loci.
  </div>
  <div>
    **Readout.** NGS to quantify integration outcomes; flow cytometry to measure functional expression.
  </div>
</TwoColumn>

I also explored **promoterless GFP** integration into T cells as a clean functional readout — knock-in only produces signal when integrated in-frame at the target locus, which removes a class of false positives that traditional GFP reporters carry.

<Figure caption="Placeholder — NGS-based integration rate comparison, blunt vs. 3'-overhang dsDNA donors." />

The result was a meaningful boost in knock-in efficiency validated across multiple loci.

## 2022 — Autoimmune team: CAR-T optimization

The Autoimmune internship was earlier in the pipeline — engineering CAR-T cells with different **costimulatory domain variations** and measuring how those changes affected targeting and persistence.

- Generated CAR-T cell lines with engineered costimulatory variants.
- Co-cultured with target cancer cell lines and quantified killing via flow cytometry.
- Used **digital droplet PCR (ddPCR)** for precise quantification of integration and copy number.

<Figure caption="Placeholder — flow cytometry of CAR-T mediated killing, by costimulatory variant." />

The work fed into the team's understanding of which costimulatory architectures generalized best across target indications.
