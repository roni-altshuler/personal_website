## Problem

The tumor microenvironment of breast cancer is heterogeneous at scales smaller than bulk transcriptomics can resolve. Where, exactly, are the cells of each type sitting? Which genes change expression with proximity to tumor cells vs. immune infiltrate vs. stromal regions?

Spatial transcriptomics promises that answer, but it bottlenecks on the same problem as the [CZ Biohub work](/work-experience/cz-biohub): you need cell segmentation accurate enough that the downstream spatial analyses aren't dominated by noise from boundary errors.

## Approach

I built a **custom Cellpose 2.0 model** trained on imaging data from a human breast cancer model, then layered spatial analysis on top of the resulting cell-level transcript counts.

<MetricCallout value="84%" label="Precision" />
<MetricCallout value="90%" label="Recall" />

With segmentation working, I:

- Performed **spatial differential expression** to surface genes whose expression depended on cellular neighborhood rather than just cell type.
- Ran **UMAP clustering** to identify cell-type subpopulations and their spatial distributions.
- Used **Squidpy** for neighborhood-graph analyses and **Scanpy** for the single-cell side.

## Connection to the CZ Biohub work

This project is what made the CZ Biohub role possible. The pattern was the same:

1. Image-based spatial assay.
2. Out-of-the-box segmentation insufficient.
3. Train a custom Cellpose model.
4. Build downstream analyses that trust the segmentation.

The Biohub work pushed the same approach to whole-embryo MERFISH at much larger scale and lifted F1 to [85%](/work-experience/cz-biohub).
